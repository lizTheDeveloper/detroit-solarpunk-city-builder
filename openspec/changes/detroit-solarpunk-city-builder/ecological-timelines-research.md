## 1. Food Forest Productivity Timeline

### General Food Forest Stages (Temperate Climate / Zone 6a-6b)

| Phase | Real Years | In-Game Turns | What Happens |
|-------|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| Establishment | 0-2 | 0-8 | Soil building, heavy mulching, annuals fill gaps. Ground cover and herbaceous layers produce small yields. |
| Early Production | 2-3 | 8-12 | Berry bushes fruit (raspberries, currants, gooseberries). Shrub layer becomes productive. |
| Transition | 3-5 | 12-20 | Smaller fruit trees begin producing (dwarf apple, cherry, pear). System begins self-maintaining — weeds suppressed by shade and mulch. |
| Fruit Tree Production | 5-7 | 20-28 | Standard fruit trees reach bearing age. System functions as ecosystem — less maintenance required. |
| Full Maturity | 7-15 | 28-60 | Canopy layer (nut trees) produces. All 7 layers fully functional. Maximum caloric output per sq ft exceeds conventional garden. |

**Key number**: A food forest takes **3-5 years** to produce meaningful harvests and **7-10 years** to reach full production.

### Detroit-Specific Food Forest / Urban Farm Examples

**Oakland Avenue Urban Farm (North End)**
- Founded: 2000 by Rev. Bertha L. Carter
- Now 25 years old (celebrating in 2025)
- Grows 33+ varieties of vegetables and fruit using organic practices
- Took approximately 3-5 years to become a "stabilizing force" in the neighborhood
- Source: [WDET: Oakland Avenue Urban Farm Turns 25](https://wdet.org/2025/09/08/the-metro-oakland-avenue-urban-farm-turns-25/)

**D-Town Farm (Rouge Park)**
- Founded: 2008 by Detroit Black Community Food Security Network
- 7 acres — the largest urban farm in Detroit
- Grows 30-36 different fruits, vegetables, and herbs
- Uses hoop houses for season extension, composting, rainwater retention, solar energy, beekeeping
- Took ~3 years to reach meaningful production scale from a standing start
- Source: [DBCFSN D-Town Farm](https://www.dbcfsn.org/dtownfarm)

**Brightmoor Farmway (Northwest Detroit)**
- Movement began: 2006 with one single garden
- By 2010-2014: Expansion into a 15-21 block "Farmway" microneighborhood
- Timeline from single garden to neighborhood transformation: ~8 years

- Source: [Planet Detroit: Urban Farming Brightmoor](https://planetdetroit.org/2025/05/urban-farming-brightmoor-detroit/)

### Game Design Implication
- **Food forest project**: First yield at turn 8 (year 2), meaningful production by turn 12-16 (years 3-4), full maturity turn 28+ (year 7+)
- **Neighborhood transformation via agriculture**: Requires 8-10 years (32-40 turns) for the "Brightmoor effect" — area identity shift

## 2. Rain Garden Water Management Improvement

### Performance Metrics (Immediate to 3 years)

| Timeframe | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Day 1 | Rain garden captures and infiltrates water during first storm event. Drains within 1 hour. |
| Year 1 | 30% more water infiltration vs. conventional lawn. Volume reduction of 56% (with underdrain) to 89% (without underdrain). |
| Year 3 | Plants fully established (especially in clay soil). Root systems at full depth. Peak performance reached. |
| Year 3+ | Self-maintaining. Ongoing 30-40% improvement over standard lawn for stormwater absorption. |

**Key number**: Rain gardens show **immediate measurable benefit** but reach full performance at **year 3** when plants establish deep root systems.

### Design Capacity Context (Detroit)
- Detroit's sewer system designed for: **1.7 inches/hour** or **3.31 inches in 24 hours**
- Actual storm events regularly exceed this (2021 storm: 6 inches in 3-5 hours)
- Rain gardens are one mitigation for the gap between design capacity and actual precipitation

Sources:
- [EPA: Bioretention Rain Gardens BMP](https://www.epa.gov/system/files/documents/2021-11/bmp-bioretention-rain-gardens.pdf)
- [EPA: Soak Up the Rain](https://www.epa.gov/soakuptherain/soak-rain-rain-gardens)
- [Virginia Tech: Stormwater Management Fact Sheet](https://www.pubs.ext.vt.edu/SPES/SPES-13/SPES-13.html)
- [ASCE: Fines Accumulation in Storm-Water Rain Garden Nine Years Post-Construction](https://ascelibrary.org/doi/10.1061/(ASCE)IR.1943-4774.0000264)

### Game Design Implication
- **Rain garden project**: Immediate partial benefit (turn 1), full benefit by turn 12 (year 3)
- Could provide "flood resistance" stat from turn 1, scaling to full value by turn 12

## 3. Detroit Soil Remediation Timelines

### Method Comparison

| Method | Timeline | Cost | Notes |
|--------|----------|------|-------|
| Excavation & removal | 6-18 months | $$$$ | Fastest. Dig out contaminated soil, truck to landfill. Most common in Detroit. |
| Soil vapor extraction | 1-3 years | $$$ | For volatile organic compounds. Pumps out contaminated vapors. |
| Bioremediation (in-situ) | 2-5 years | $$ | Microorganisms break down contaminants. |
| Phytoremediation | 5-10 years | $ | Plants extract contaminants. Cheapest but slowest. Sunflowers, willows, poplar trees. |
| Capping / containment | 3-6 months | $$ | Doesn't remove contamination — seals it in place. |

### Detroit-Specific Brownfield Projects

**Piquette Square (Former Studebaker Plant)**
- Contamination: Industrial chemicals + fire damage
- Remediation: Underground storage tank removal + vapor intrusion barrier installation
- Investment: $23 million
- Opened: June 2010
- Source: [EPA: Brownfield Redevelopment Detroit](https://www.epa.gov/brownfields/brownfield-redevelopment-detroit)

**Cardinal Health Medical Warehouse (New Center, 18 acres / 98 parcels)**
- Contamination: Contaminated soil, underground storage tanks
- Remediation: Soil vapor ventilation system, tank removal
- Investment: $30 million
- Source: [EPA: Brownfield Redevelopment Detroit](https://www.epa.gov/brownfields/brownfield-redevelopment-detroit)

**Riverside Park (Parcel 3)**
- Contamination: Contaminated soil
- Remediation: Soil removal
- Investment: $786,742 (assessment + cleanup grants)
- Source: [EPA: Brownfield Redevelopment Detroit](https://www.epa.gov/brownfields/brownfield-redevelopment-detroit)

**Outdoor Adventure Center (Globe Building, Detroit Riverfront)**
- Contamination: Contaminated soils from industrial dry dock
- Remediation: $200,000 soil removal, $15M total renovation
- Result: 100,000 visitors in first year (2016)
- Source: [EPA: Brownfield Redevelopment Detroit](https://www.epa.gov/brownfields/brownfield-redevelopment-detroit)

**City-wide stats**: Detroit Brownfield Redevelopment Authority established 1998, has facilitated over 250 brownfield redevelopment plans. Environmental Affairs has managed EGLE grants since 2018 totaling over $4 million across 18 projects.

Sources:
- [City of Detroit: Brownfields Redevelopment](https://detroitmi.gov/departments/buildings-safety-engineering-and-environmental-department-bseed/bseed-divisions/environmental-affairs/brownfields-redevelopment)
- [EPA: Brownfield Redevelopment in Detroit](https://www.epa.gov/brownfields/brownfield-redevelopment-detroit)
- [Planet Detroit: $2M Grants for Michigan Brownfield Cleanup](https://planetdetroit.org/2025/12/brownfields-cleanup-michigan-funding/)

### Game Design Implication
- **Excavation project**: Complete in 4-6 turns (1-1.5 years). Expensive but fast.
- **Phytoremediation project**: Takes 20-40 turns (5-10 years). Cheap, creates green space during process, but land unusable for food production until complete.
- Player choice: spend resources for quick fix vs. invest in slow green remediation

## 5. Native Planting / Ecological Corridor Restoration

### Timeline to Measurable Biodiversity Improvement

| Timeframe | Measurable Change |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Year 1 | Native plants established. Increased insect populations (pollinators arrive). |
| Year 2-3 | Plants reach full bloom cycle. 15% increase in urban biodiversity (pollinators + small mammals). Bird species begin returning. |
| Year 3-5 | 15-40% increase in species diversity. Complex ecosystem establishing. Nesting birds documented. |
| Year 5-10 | Corridor effect: species movement between habitat patches. Self-sustaining ecosystem. |
| Year 10-20 | Mature ecological corridor. Full food web restored. Measurable improvements in surrounding areas. |

### Detroit-Specific Biodiversity Projects

**Detroit Bird City Project (Callahan Park + others)**
- Started: Spring 2019 (planting at Callahan Park)
- Partners: Detroit Bird Alliance, City of Detroit, USFWS, Detroit Zoo
- Results by 2024 (5 years in):
  - 45+ acres of meadow habitat created (20 planted, 25 more underway)
  - 100+ bird species recorded during monitoring at Callahan Park
  - Species include: indigo buntings, scarlet tanagers, gray catbirds, chipping sparrows, American goldfinches
  - 39 native flower types + 6 native grass species planted
- Timeline: Plants needed **2-3 years to fully grow** before blooming fully
- Source: [Planet Detroit: Long-term Fight for Biodiversity](https://planetdetroit.org/2020/06/in-detroit-the-long-term-fight-for-biodiversity-is-bearing-fruit-and-flowers-and-birds-and-butterflies/)

**Rouge Park Restoration (Friends of Rouge Park)**
- Founded: 2002
- Results by 2020 (18 years in):
  - 40 acres of restored prairie (maintained via prescribed burns)
  - Great blue herons: expanded to 50 nests along Rouge River
  - Wild turkeys, coyotes, hooded mergansers, deer returned
  - Pileated woodpeckers documented in Lahser Marsh
  - Bald eagles sighted for 2 consecutive years
  - Eastern meadowlark nesting
- Source: [Planet Detroit: Biodiversity in Detroit](https://planetdetroit.org/2020/06/in-detroit-the-long-term-fight-for-biodiversity-is-bearing-fruit-and-flowers-and-birds-and-butterflies/)

**Detroit Bee City USA (2024)**
- City committed to pollinator conservation
- Pollinator habitat kits distributed (Xerces Society partnership)
- Detroit Hives leading apiary initiatives
- Source: [City of Detroit: Bee City USA Initiative](https://detroitmi.gov/news/city-commits-pollinator-conservation-joins-bee-city-usa-initiative-led-detroit-hives)

Sources:
- [Xerces Society: Detroit Pollinator Habitat Kits](https://xerces.org/pollinator-conservation/habitat-kits/detroit)
- [PMC: Assessing Methods for Native Plants on Urban Vacant Land](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7882654/)

### Game Design Implication
- **Native planting project**: First pollinators arrive turn 4 (year 1). Measurable bird diversity by turn 8-12 (years 2-3). Full corridor effect by turn 20+ (year 5+).
- **Cascading bonus**: Nearby tiles with native plantings should create corridor synergy — the more connected green tiles, the faster biodiversity improves.

## 6. Great Lakes Climate Data: Detroit Extreme Weather

### Flooding

| Event | Rainfall | Impact |
|-------|----------|--------|
| August 2014 | Major flooding | Widespread basement flooding |
| 2016 | Heavy rainfall event | Basement flooding |
| 2019 | Extreme precipitation | Infrastructure overwhelmed |
| 2020 | Heavy rainfall | Basement flooding |

### Game Design Implication
- **Climate events should increase in frequency** over game progression (solarpunk framing: you're building resilience against worsening conditions)
- **Flood events**: Major flood every 2-3 years (8-12 turns). Minor flooding annually.
- **Heat waves**: 1-2 per summer (every 4 turns). Worse if lacking tree canopy / green space.
- **Ice storms**: Every 3-5 years (12-20 turns). Knock out power if grid not resilient.
- **Player counter**: Rain gardens reduce flood damage. Tree canopy reduces heat. Solar + battery resists ice storm outage.

## Summary: Eco-Health Progression Rate Calibration

| System | Time to First Effect | Time to Full Effect | Suggested Turn Model |
|--------|---------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Rain garden | Immediate | 3 years (12 turns) | Linear ramp, 25% per year |
| Native planting | 1 year (4 turns) | 5-10 years (20-40 turns) | Slow exponential with corridor bonus |
| Food forest | 2 years (8 turns) | 7-10 years (28-40 turns) | Staged unlocks at years 2, 4, 7 |
| Tree canopy | 3 years (12 turns) | 15+ years (60+ turns) | Very slow linear (2%/year) |
| Solar installation | 18-24 months (6-8 turns) | Operational at completion | Binary: under construction vs. operational |
| Soil remediation (excavation) | 6-18 months (2-6 turns) | Complete at end | Binary: contaminated vs. clean |
| Soil remediation (phyto) | 5 years partial (20 turns) | 10 years (40 turns) | Gradual, creates green space meanwhile |
| Biodiversity corridor | 2-3 years (8-12 turns) | 10-20 years (40-80 turns) | Accelerates with adjacent green tiles |

### Recommended "Eco Health" Formula Principles

1. **Nothing is instant** — even rain gardens need a season to show results
2. **Stacking accelerates** — food forest + native planting + rain garden on adjacent tiles should create synergy bonuses
3. **Climate events test resilience** — floods/heat/ice storms reveal gaps in your ecological infrastructure
4. **Early wins, long tail** — players should see SOMETHING happen in 2-4 turns, but full ecosystem maturity takes 20-40+ turns
5. **Maintenance matters** — without maintenance investment, gains decay (Detroit loses 2,000 trees/year)
