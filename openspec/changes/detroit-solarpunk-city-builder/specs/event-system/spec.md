## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: Events present choices with specific consequences
Events SHALL present the player with a situation and 2-3 response options. Each option has defined numeric consequences.

#### Scenario: Event with choices
- **WHEN** a "Water Main Break" event triggers on a tile
- **THEN** the player sees the event description and options:
  - (A) Emergency repair: -$0.3M budget, no lasting change
  - (B) Replace with green infrastructure: -$0.5M budget, requires Will >= 30%, tile eco +8%, flood adaptation +20%
  - (C) Community-led response: -$0.15M budget, +2% Trust, +1 turn delay before fix completes (tile is damaged for 1 additional turn)

### Requirement: Event types with defined generation rules
The system SHALL support event types generated by specific conditions:

**Climate Events** (generated by climate-system, see climate-system spec for probabilities):
- Heat Wave: probability based on season (Summer) and climate pressure
- Flooding: probability based on season (Spring) and climate pressure
- Severe Storm: probability based on season (Fall) and climate pressure
- Ice Storm: probability based on season (Winter) and climate pressure

**Political Events** (generated by political context):
- State Legislature Pushback: 0.05/turn (0.08 in Restoration+), requires 3+ policies enacted
- Federal Grant Opportunity: 0.06/turn, offers +$0.5M budget at cost of -3% Will (strings attached)
- Developer Proposal: 0.08/turn, offers +$0.3M budget but triggers gentrification risk on 1 tile unless Land Trust present

**Community Events** (generated by Trust level):
- Neighborhood Request: 0.10/turn when Trust > 40%, specific tile requests a project type (completing it within 4 turns gives +3% Trust bonus)
- Mutual Aid Moment: 0.05/turn when Trust > 60%, +$0.3M budget, +1% Trust
- Cultural Celebration: 0.04/turn when Trust > 50%, +2% Trust, +1% Will

**Crisis Events** (generated by meter thresholds):
- Water Shutoff: 0.08/turn when Budget < $1.0M, -3% Trust, -2% Will
- Infrastructure Failure: 0.06/turn when Eco < 25%, -$0.2M budget, -5% tile eco on random tile
- Public Health Emergency: 0.04/turn when Climate > 60%, -2% Trust, -$0.3M budget

#### Scenario: Climate event generated by system
- **WHEN** the climate system determines a heat wave occurs (Summer, rolled probability succeeded)
- **THEN** a heat wave event with severity based on climate pressure is placed in the event queue

#### Scenario: Political event from context
- **WHEN** the player has 4 enacted policies and is in Transition stage
- **THEN** State Legislature Pushback has 0.05 probability per turn of firing, draining -5.5% Will if it does

### Requirement: Event frequency caps and cooldowns
The event system SHALL enforce these limits to prevent event flooding:

**Per-turn caps:**
- Maximum 3 events per turn (across all types)
- Maximum 1 crisis event per turn
- Maximum 1 climate event per turn

**Cooldowns:**
- Same event type cannot fire within 3 turns of its last occurrence (e.g., if a Heat Wave fires on Turn 5, the next Heat Wave cannot fire until Turn 8+)
- Crisis events have a 4-turn cooldown per type
- Re-election events are exempt from cooldowns (they fire at fixed turns)

**Priority when more events trigger than the cap allows:**
1. Crisis events (highest priority)
2. Climate events
3. Political events
4. Community events (lowest priority, deferred to next turn if space allows)

#### Scenario: Event cap limits events
- **WHEN** a crisis event, a climate event, and 2 political events all trigger on the same turn
- **THEN** the crisis event, climate event, and 1 political event fire (3 total, cap reached). The second political event is suppressed for this turn.

#### Scenario: Cooldown prevents repeat events
- **WHEN** a Heat Wave fired on Turn 12 and the system rolls for a Heat Wave on Turn 14
- **THEN** the Heat Wave is suppressed (cooldown: 3 turns, next eligible Turn 15). Other climate event types can still fire.

### Requirement: Event consequences with immediate and delayed effects
Event responses SHALL have immediate numeric effects and may have delayed effects:

#### Scenario: Delayed consequence
- **WHEN** the player chooses the community-led response (C) to a water crisis
- **THEN** Community Trust increases by +2% immediately AND a follow-up "Community Water Cooperative" event has a 0.40 probability of triggering 2-3 turns later, offering: +3% Trust, +2% Food Sovereignty, and the tile gains permanent water quality bonus (+5% eco)

#### Scenario: Developer proposal consequences
- **WHEN** a Developer Proposal event fires and the player accepts the funding (+$0.3M)
- **THEN** budget increases by $0.3M immediately. On the next turn, there is a 0.50 probability of a "Gentrification Pressure" follow-up event on the affected tile: -3% Trust unless a Land Trust project is completed on that tile.

### Requirement: Re-election as multi-turn arc
Re-election SHALL be a multi-turn arc, not a binary check. It triggers at Turn 15 (one turn before the term ends) and resolves at Turn 16.

**Turn 15 (Campaign Turn):**
- A "Campaign Season" event fires, showing the player their current standing:
  - Trust >= 40% and Will >= 25%: "Strong position. Re-election likely."
  - Trust >= 20% and Will >= 12%: "Contested. Campaign actions will be critical."
  - Below either threshold: "Crisis. Re-election is at serious risk."
- The player gets +2 bonus narrative actions this turn (on top of their normal allocation) specifically for campaigning
- Campaign-specific narrative actions available:
  - Rally: +3% Will, +1% Trust (campaign-only action)
  - Promise: +2% Will but queues a "Broken Promise" event for Turn 20 if the chosen meter does not improve by 5% within 4 turns
  - Coalition Building: +2% Trust, +1% Will

**Turn 16 (Election Turn):**
- Re-election check: Trust >= 20% AND Will >= 12%
- If passed: new term begins, +5% Will bonus (mandate), +$0.5M budget bonus (transition funding)
- If failed: game over with an epilogue showing what the player accomplished and what was lost

#### Scenario: Re-election campaign arc
- **WHEN** Turn 15 arrives with Trust at 35% and Will at 18%
- **THEN** the Campaign Season event fires showing "Contested" status. The player receives 2 bonus narrative actions. If they use Rally (+3% Will) and Coalition Building (+2% Trust), Will rises to 21% and Trust to 37%. On Turn 16, Trust (37%) >= 20% and Will (21%) >= 12%, so re-election succeeds.

#### Scenario: Re-election failure
- **WHEN** Turn 16 arrives with Trust at 18% and Will at 10%
- **THEN** re-election fails. The game ends with: "The people of Detroit chose a different path. Your legacy: [summary of tiles transformed, policies enacted, meters achieved]. The work continues, even without you."

### Requirement: Scripted story events at milestones
The system SHALL include scripted events at specific milestones:

| Milestone | Turn/Condition | Event |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| First project completion | First project finishes | "First Victory": +2% Trust, tutorial about project effects |
| First tile transformation | First tile reaches 40% eco | "Signs of Life": +1% Trust, +1% Will, visual celebration |
| 5 tiles transformed | 5 tiles at transition+ | "Momentum": +2% Will, narrative about community noticing change |
| Re-election 1 | Turn 15-16 | Campaign arc (see above) |
| Stage: Transition | Stage transition fires | "A New Chapter": +3% Trust, +2% Will, new projects narrated |
| Tipping Point 1 | Climate reaches 60% | "The Warning": -2% Trust (fear), climate tutorial |
| 15 tiles transformed | 15 tiles at transition+ | "The Tipping Point of Hope": +3% Will, media attention |
| Re-election 2 | Turn 31-32 | Campaign arc |
| Stage: Restoration | Stage transition fires | "Restoration Begins": +5% Trust, +3% Will |
| Tipping Point 2 | Climate reaches 85% | "The Crisis Deepens": -3% Trust, -2% Will, urgency |
| Re-election 3 | Turn 47-48 | Campaign arc |
| Stage: Beyond the Map | Stage transition fires | "Beyond Detroit": regional view unlocks, +5% Will |
| Re-election 4 | Turn 63-64 | Final campaign arc + endgame summary |

#### Scenario: First project milestone event
- **WHEN** the player completes their first project ever
- **THEN** a scripted "First Victory" event triggers: +2% Trust, and a narrative moment explains how project effects work and connect to meters

### Requirement: Event queue with priority ordering
Events SHALL queue and present in order of priority: Crisis > Climate > Political > Community. The player MUST respond to crisis events before taking other turn actions. Non-crisis events can be deferred (responded to at any point during the player action phase).

#### Scenario: Crisis event takes priority
- **WHEN** both a Water Shutoff (crisis) and a Cultural Celebration (community) event trigger on the same turn
- **THEN** the Water Shutoff event is presented first and must be resolved before the player can access Projects, Policy, or Narrative panels. The Cultural Celebration is presented after but can be deferred.
