# Playtest Report: Solarpunk Detroit

**Date:** May 4, 2026
**Tester:** Gemini CLI
**Turns Played:** 40
**Stage Reached:** Transition

## 1. Final Result
- **Result:** Survived 40 turns, safely passed first re-election at Turn 16.
- **Stage:** Reached **Transition** stage around Turn 4. Remained in Transition until the end.
- **Policies Enacted:** 8 (all available policies by Turn 32).
- **Budget:** $13.1M.
- **Meters:**
  - Community Trust: 93%
  - Eco Health: 30%
  - Food Sovereignty: 67%
  - Political Will: 57%
  - Climate Pressure: 56%

## 2. Turn-by-Turn Progression (Key Milestones)
- **Turn 1:** Started with $1.5M. Funded Community Land Trust (Corktown), Food Forest (Brightmoor), and two Rain Gardens. Trust at 50%.
- **Turn 4:** Reached **Transition** stage. Trust 75%, Food 34%.
- **Turn 6:** Enacted first two policies: "Urban Agriculture Zoning" and "Green Infrastructure Grants".
- **Turn 16:** Re-election. Successfully passed with high trust and multiple projects completed.
- **Turn 17:** Budget jumped to $4.5M. Focus shifted heavily to restoration-tier projects (Wetland Restoration, Wildlife Corridor).
- **Turn 32:** Budget at $9.6M. Enacted all remaining policies. Eco Health struggling with decay (stuck around 30-33%).
- **Turn 40:** Finished session. Eco Health at 30% despite massive investment in high-impact projects.

## 3. Key Strategic Decisions
- **Early Land Trusts:** Prioritized funding the Community Land Trust in Corktown in Turn 1 to mitigate gentrification. This proved effective as Corktown's gentrification pressure remained manageable compared to other areas like Indian Village.
- **Eco-Spam Strategy:** Attempted to combat Eco decay by blanketing neighborhoods with Rain Gardens and eventually high-tier Restoration projects.
- **Policy Aggression:** Spent Political Will as soon as thresholds were met to unlock long-term bonuses, especially the cost reduction for ecology projects.
- **Event Management:** Consistently chose options that preserved Political Will or boosted Trust, even at the cost of budget, leading to the massive Trust and Will reserves in the late game.

## 4. What Felt Good / Bad
- **Good:**
  - The "Transition" stage felt like a real achievement.
  - The synergy between policies and project costs was satisfying.
  - The unique character dialogue (when present) added great flavor and Detroit context.
- **Bad:**
  - **Eco Decay:** The rate of ecological decay felt extremely high. Even with multiple "Restoration" tier projects active, the Eco meter often ticked down or stayed flat. Reaching the 55% requirement for the "Restoration" stage felt almost impossible.
  - **Budget Explosion:** Late-game budget became trivial ($13M+), removing the tension of financial decisions.

## 5. Balance Issues
- **Budget Scaling:** By Turn 20, the budget seems to scale exponentially or receive massive injections that make cost irrelevant.
- **Eco Difficulty:** There is a significant gap between the ease of reaching the Transition stage (Trust/Food) and the difficulty of reaching the Restoration stage (Eco).
- **Gentrification:** Land Trusts are almost too effective at 100% blocking gentrification, while areas without them spike very quickly.

## 6. UI/UX Issues
- **Dialogue Bug:** Grace Okafor-Williams and Kezia Monroe share identical dialogue lines for "Community Kitchen" and "Community Land Trust" starts ("Okay so *finally* someone gets it...").
- **Conversation Persistence:** Conversation panels often remain open and overlap other UI elements, requiring multiple clicks to clear.
- **Events Tab:** The "End Turn" button being disabled due to pending events doesn't always automatically navigate you to the Events tab, which can be confusing for new players.
- **Top Bar Info:** The "4 Projects Max" label is useful but could be clearer if it showed "Current/Max" (e.g., "3/5 Projects").

## 7. Suggestions for Improvement
- **Adjust Eco Decay:** Lower the base decay rate or increase the per-turn contribution of finished Eco projects.
- **Add Budget Sinks:** Introduce late-game maintenance costs or "Mega-Projects" that require the multi-million dollar reserves.
- **Leader Progression:** Leaders could offer more complex multi-stage proposals as Trust grows.
- **Visual Feedback:** More visual "solarpunk" transformation on the neighborhood tiles as Eco health improves.
