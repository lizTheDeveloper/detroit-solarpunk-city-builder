# Gemini Fun Review

Date: 2026-05-06T01:35:25.765Z
Playtest: 34 turns

This was a fascinating run. I ended with a "perfect" city on paper—100% Ecology, 100% Food, and 85% Trust—yet I was unceremoniously booted out of office. It turns out that fixing the planet is easy; it’s the people and the politics that'll kill you.

Here is my report as both a player and a game designer.

---

### **Scorecard**

1.  **Fun Factor: 7/10**
    The engine-building aspect of reclaiming lots and stacking community projects is very satisfying. However, the late-game "action starvation" (constantly seeing 0/2 actions) felt like I was watching my own funeral in slow motion rather than playing a game.

2.  **Tension & Drama: 9/10**
    The "Detroit Politics" simulation is spot-on. Seeing my meters at 100% while the election predictor told me I was a failure (30/45) created a unique kind of stress. It felt like the "activist's dilemma"—doing the right work on the ground but losing the narrative at City Hall.

3.  **Meaningful Choices: 9/10**
    The Crisis Forks are the soul of the game. Choosing the Community Land Trust (CLT) over the bulk sale (T6, T27) felt like a high-stakes values test. It wasn't just "which number goes up?" but "what kind of city am I building?"

4.  **Pacing: 5/10**
    The early game is tight and engaging. The mid-game (T15-T30) is a great "climb." But the end-game felt broken. I hit 100% Eco/Food way too early (Turn 31), and the final 15 turns were spent waiting for budget or actions that never came.

5.  **Theme Integration: 10/10**
    The terminology (Brightmoor, DTE, CLT, Soil Remediation) and the specific tension between community-led solar and corporate grids make this feel like a Detroit story, not just a generic city-builder.

---

### **Detailed Analysis**

#### **6. The Single Most Interesting Decision**
**The T27 Community Land Trust (CLT) vs. Bulk Land Purchase.**
I was already broke. Choosing the CLT was objectively "bad" for my budget, but it felt like the only way to protect the wins I’d made in the North End. It was a choice between **Systems Maintenance** (Budget) and **Values Integrity** (Anti-displacement). I chose the latter, and while it contributed to my eventual loss, it’s the part of the story I remember most.

#### **7. What felt broken, unfun, or tedious?**
*   **The Action Bottleneck:** In the late game, I almost never had "Main Actions" (Narrative/Policy) available. Seeing `0/2 actions remaining` at the start of Turn 14, 23, 24, 29, 42, and 46 was incredibly frustrating. I felt like I was being punished for something I didn't understand. If those actions are being consumed by "active projects" or "crises," that needs to be much clearer.
*   **Political Will (The Silent Killer):** I lost because I couldn't reach the "Restoration" stage. I had the meters (100% Eco/Food), but I only had 1 policy enacted because my Political Will was perpetually stuck around 10%. Without Will, I couldn't pass the second policy required for the stage gate. It felt like the "Ecology" victory was too easy, while the "Political" victory was nearly impossible.
*   **UI/Interpolation Bugs:** Seeing my budget displayed as `/bin/shK` on Turn 46 was a bit jarring.

#### **8. What would make you want to play again?**
I want to know if a "Corporate/Pragmatist" run is actually easier. If I sided with DTE and the bulk land buyers, would I have the Political Will and Budget to pass more policies? I'd play again to see if the game has a "bad path" that is actually a smoother gameplay experience, which would be a brilliant (if cynical) commentary on real-world politics.

#### **9. One-sentence pitch**
"A political survival simulator where you try to build a green utopia in Detroit, only to realize that fixing the soil is much easier than fixing the City Council."

---

### **Design Feedback for the Devs**
*   **Fix the Stage Gates:** Having 100% Ecology and 100% Food but being stuck in "Transition" (Stage 2 of 4) feels wrong. The meters should probably scale or decay faster, or the "Policy" requirement needs more levers than just a trickle of Political Will.
*   **Action Economy:** Clarify *why* the player has 0/2 actions. If it's because the council is blocking me or I'm over-extended on projects, tell me! Don't just grey out the buttons.
*   **Leader Relations:** The disconnect between "High Total Trust" and "Council Hatred" is a great mechanic, but there aren't enough clear ways to "lobby" or "gift" to specific leaders other than waiting for their random proposals. We need a way to spend Budget or Will to directly influence a disgruntled leader.
